Spotlight Women

Welcome to the Spotlight Women page! Here we have interviews with accomplished women who offer advice and share their experiences in the workplace.

Click on the image for more!
"I love my job. There is no substitute for that as a motivating factor." -Lisa Opoku "One key word I always have in mind is "motivation". Motivation provides energy and joy to accomplish the goal and with the drive and passion, one would willingly put effort towards the goal, which is critical for success. " -Jee Hyun Wang " My biggest challenge at work has definitely been confidence and “imposter syndrome.” I constantly have to remind myself that there is no such thing as a pity hire, that I was hired for a reason and that I can actively contribute to the success of my team and the firm." -Melissa Carey
"I have been most satisfied with the help and constant support I’ve gotten from my teammates. I’m never afraid to ask questions because I know no one in my team will be annoyed or judge me for not knowing something! On top of that, I came in thinking that everything would be about work, work, work and any distractions would be frowned upon! But this wasn’t true!" -Irma Nomani "Sometimes it can be hard to see our own biases, but that’s where we begin in trying to remedy them. A big part of the issues that women or minorities face in CS, in particular, I don’t think comes from people who are outright discriminatory. I think a big part of it comes from subconscious or institutional bias – i.e. people are discriminating not because they truly believe women aren’t as good at CS, but just because they don’t realize they’re even doing it. " -Lauren O'Connor " Shout-Out to A.R.C.K!" -Emily Lobel
"As a teaching assistant for the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program, it has been a delight to see the development of the girls' technical skills and character as they've worked alongside people who were once strangers but are now a support system." -Hannah He "Everything I do is with passion, dedication, rigor and purpose. It's important for me to believe in my work and set a high bar for myself." -Sandra Anojulu "My friend had asked me if I would want to be a part of making a blog with her, and the reason why I became so inspired and so passionate about creating the blog is because there isn’t really a space for women of color in STEM and often times I feel like they are an ignored demographic as people. The team and I feel that creating a space that can inspire, encourage and uplift, other young women in the STEM industry." -Ademusoyo Awosika-Olumo
